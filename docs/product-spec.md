## Problem Statement

Nicolas Lacombe has built a cult-like community of 8,000 people paying €70 each to
work silently together in Discord voice rooms and track their hours in a Notion template.
€15k/month revenue from a product he describes as "crappy." The community wants the
desktop app. The question is: what does Miron build before Paris (April 7-9), and what
do they align on as the real v1 together?

Secondary question: does the product clarity confirm that this is the right cofounder
match, and at what equity split?

## Demand Evidence

- 8,000 Discord community members, many paying €69.99 one-time
- €15k/month recurring revenue from Notion template + Discord
- 361K views on Twitter promo video of a desktop app that doesn't exist yet
- Users cooking 400 hours/month in voice rooms — gym-level retention, not app-level

## Status Quo

Users currently: join a Discord voice room (silent co-working), work for a session,
leave the voice room, receive an automated Discord message syncing their hours to a
Notion dashboard. The Notion dashboard tracks total hours, rank, leaderboard position,
skill tree (manually updated), and habits.

Pain: manual input kills the habit. Two clicks to start, three to end, then update
Notion manually. The voice room accountability is real. The data capture is broken.

The workaround costs users ~5-10 minutes of friction per session. At 400 hours/month
(some power users), that's hours of wasted time just logging.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user:** 18-30 year old, remote worker or student, already knows about focus
techniques (Pomodoro, deep work), feels isolated working alone, wants to be the kind
of person who works hard and has something to show for it. NOT an enterprise user.
NOT someone who needs features explained. Someone who watched the promo video and
thought "I want to be that person."

**Narrowest wedge (prototype before Paris):**
Electron desktop app with four things:
1. Focus timer (start/stop, visible on screen, can float on top of other windows)
2. Task field (what are you working on right now — one line)
3. XP counter (session ends → XP earned based on duration)
4. Live leaderboard (see where you rank against community this week)

That's it. No AI, no quests, no hero's journey, no skill tree. Just the Discord
voice room + Notion dashboard replaced in one app, zero friction.

**Auth for prototype:** Discord OAuth or magic link email (Supabase). Existing
Discord community members sign in with Discord to preserve their identity and
leaderboard rank. New users get email signup. This is the simplest path that
preserves continuity for the 8,000 existing users.

**Prototype scope boundary:** Leaderboard requires Supabase realtime. Auth requires
Supabase. Both are in scope for the prototype — without them, the social layer doesn't
exist and the prototype proves nothing interesting. Timer + XP without leaderboard is
just a stopwatch.

**On closing the laptop mid-session:** Session pauses automatically when lid closes
(Electron power event). User is prompted on wake: "Resume session?" XP is only awarded
for confirmed completed sessions, not abandoned ones.

**Real v1 (to align on in Paris):**
The identity layer. Passive tracking (active window detection), hero's journey
onboarding (human design quiz, quest assignment, first call to adventure), and the
full emotional experience Nicolas described. 3-4 weeks post-Paris.

## Premises

1. **The retention problem is already solved.** 8,000 Discord users with cult-like
   behavior means v1 just needs to not break what Discord built. Don't reinvent the
   community — migrate it.

2. **This is a social identity system, not a productivity tool.** Features that serve
   identity (rank, XP, streak, community presence) are load-bearing. Features that are
   purely functional (integrations, AI coach, analytics) are decoration in v1.

3. **Passive tracking must be in Real v1 (post-Paris), not the prototype.** Manual
   input is the reason the current product feels "crappy." The prototype intentionally
   keeps manual start/stop to ship before Paris. Real v1 adds passive window detection.
   Mac requires Screen Recording permission + Apple notarization (Apple Developer
   Program, $99/year). Windows uses win32 APIs. This is real complexity — not a weekend
   task — but it's the single biggest UX improvement possible and must not be deferred
   past Real v1.

4. **Miron brings more than code.** Newsletter (2,849 subs, 80% OR), GTM instinct,
   distribution experience (Telegram, VK, Superdao), and founding team experience.
   That's worth 30-35% minimum regardless of Nicolas's head start.

## Landscape Findings

**[Layer 1]** Everyone knows gamified productivity apps have terrible retention.
Day-30 retention is 4.1% on average. Habitica, Forest, BeeDone, Focumon all face
the same retention cliff.

**[Layer 2]** Apps that beat the cliff share one trait: persistent social groups
with mutual stakes. Habitica's team damage mechanic. Focumon's multiplayer rooms.
The social layer creates asymmetric exit cost.

**[Layer 3 — EUREKA]** Promethee already solved the retention problem before building
the app. The 8,000-person Discord community IS the retention mechanism. They built
community first, app second. Every competitor does the opposite. This is the real
moat — not the gamification, not the AI, not the data. The sequence.

Sources: [Focumon](https://www.focumon.com/landing), [Habitica review](https://accompli.app/habitica-app-review/), [Retention benchmarks](https://enable3.io/blog/app-retention-benchmarks-2025)

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Discord Replacement Prototype (before Paris)
- Summary: Electron app doing exactly what Discord + Notion does today, in one click
- Effort: S (1-2 weeks) | Risk: Low
- Pros: existing users migrate immediately; shows Nicolas you can ship; validates core loop
- Cons: no passive tracking; still manual; won't feel "AAA"

### Approach B: Identity Layer — Real v1 (align on in Paris, build after)
- Summary: Passive tracking + hero's journey onboarding + full emotional experience
- Effort: M (3-4 weeks post-Paris) | Risk: Medium
- Pros: closest to actual vision; passive tracking is the key differentiator
- Cons: technically fiddly (OS permissions); needs design work; can't show working code in Paris

### Approach C: Web-first (rejected)
- Summary: Web app first, validate with existing users, build desktop after
- Effort: S | Risk: Low
- Pros: fastest feedback; can reuse Polary codebase
- Cons: conflicts with Nicolas's vision; misses "always on top" overlay; 361K people watched a desktop app

## Recommended Approach

**A as prototype, B as the real plan.**

Build Approach A before Paris (April 7). Show Nicolas a working Electron app with
the four core features. This proves you can ship fast, gives him something real to
react to, and earns the right to co-design Approach B together in Paris.

In Paris: align on Approach B as the actual v1. Agree on timeline, tech stack,
and equity before leaving.

**Tech stack:**
- Electron + React (fastest path, web skills, large ecosystem)
- SQLite for local data (sessions, XP, user profile)
- Supabase for leaderboard sync (already know it from Polary)
- Node.js OS APIs for active window detection (passive tracking — Real v1, post-Paris)

## Open Questions

1. **Equity:** Nicolas said "not 50/50 but much more than 10%." Number not defined.
   Miron's floor is 25%, target 30-35%. Needs to be resolved in Paris before building starts.

2. **Existing Discord bot/data + migration:** Can historical session data be migrated
   into the new app? If power users lose their rank and XP history on Day 1, churn
   is almost certain. This is a hard prerequisite for the beta launch, not an optional
   nice-to-have. Ask Nicolas for data format before Paris. If migration is impossible,
   the beta must show users their historical total alongside new in-app XP.

3. **Vesting:** Does Nicolas have a vesting schedule on his existing stake?
   Standard is 4-year/1-year cliff. If he doesn't, this is a red flag to resolve.

4. **EF Bridge SF terms:** Is EF investing again (250K/10%) or just providing
   network/program access? This affects post-Paris dilution math.

5. **Entity:** French company for now. Delaware flip is €10k and straightforward
   but needs to happen before a US fundraise. Timeline?

6. **Window tracking permissions:** Mac requires Screen Recording permission for
   active window detection. Windows is simpler. This needs a UX solution — users
   need to trust the app before granting it.
